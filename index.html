Contests

Modeling Opioid Crisis Based on Cellular Automata and Chi-Square Test
Finalist Winner(top 0.31%) in The Mathematical Contest in Modeling, awarded by COMAP, 2019
[Summary, Report]

We designed Opioid Spread Model based on Cellular Automata to simulate the spread of opioid between and in five states in U.S. We futher used Chi-Square tests to identify the important socio-economic determinants of drug abuse. Eventually our team was designated as Finalist Winner of this contest (top 0.31% in 25,370 teams).

Heat Conduction Model Based on Finite Difference Method
National Second Prize(top 4%) in Contemporary Undergraduate Mathematical Contest in Modeling, awarded by CSIAM, 2018
[Summary, Report]

We employed Finite Difference Method model the heat distribution data in a multilayer high temperature resistant coverall. Furthermore, we used Bisection to optimize a single layer of the overalls, and applied the Genetic Algorithm to find the optimal thickness for two layers. Eventually our team Won National Second Prize at this contest (top 4% in 38,573 teams)
